# Diode

## What is a Diode?

A diode is a semiconductor device formed by a P-N junction that allows current to flow primarily in one direction.

It acts as a one-way path for current and is widely used in protection, rectification, and switching applications.

---

## Basic Structure

A diode consists of:

* P-type semiconductor
* N-type semiconductor

When joined together, a depletion region is formed at the junction.

This depletion region creates a built-in barrier that controls current flow.

---

## Working Principle

### Forward Bias

```text
P → Positive
N → Negative
```

* Depletion region becomes thinner
* Barrier reduces
* Current flows

### Reverse Bias

```text
P → Negative
N → Positive
```

* Depletion region becomes wider
* Barrier increases
* Current is blocked (except small leakage current)

---

## Important Characteristics

### Forward Voltage Drop

Typical silicon diode:

```text
≈ 0.7 V
```

Typical germanium diode:

```text
≈ 0.3 V
```

### Reverse Leakage Current

A small current flows even during reverse bias.

### Non-Linear Device

A diode does not follow a linear voltage-current relationship.

---

## Embedded System Applications

* Reverse polarity protection
* Input protection circuits
* Power supply rectification
* Signal routing
* Voltage spike suppression

---

## Automotive Applications

### Reverse Battery Protection

Protects ECUs from incorrect battery connections.

### Flyback Protection

Used across:

* Relays
* Motors
* Solenoids
* Injectors

Prevents voltage spikes generated by inductive loads.

### Transient Protection

Helps protect automotive electronics from voltage surges and switching noise.

---

## Breakdown Region

If reverse voltage exceeds the rated limit:

* Zener breakdown (controlled in Zener diodes)
* Avalanche breakdown (undesirable in normal diodes)

---

## Comparisons

### Diode vs Resistor

| Diode                 | Resistor             |
| --------------------- | -------------------- |
| Non-linear            | Linear               |
| Directional           | Bidirectional        |
| Has threshold voltage | No threshold voltage |

### Diode vs Zener Diode

| Diode                  | Zener Diode                              |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Used for rectification | Used for voltage regulation              |
| Blocks reverse current | Conducts in controlled reverse breakdown |

## Interview Questions

### What is a diode?

A diode is a P-N junction semiconductor device that allows current flow primarily in one direction.

### Why does a diode conduct only in one direction?

Because the depletion region barrier decreases in forward bias and increases in reverse bias.

### Why is the forward voltage of a silicon diode approximately 0.7 V?

It is the voltage required to overcome the built-in potential barrier of the P-N junction.

### What is a flyback diode?

A diode connected across an inductive load to suppress voltage spikes when current is switched OFF.

### Why are diodes important in automotive ECUs?

They provide protection against reverse battery connection, voltage transients, and inductive kickback.

---

## Summary

A diode is a P-N junction device that controls current flow using the depletion region barrier. It is widely used in embedded and automotive systems for protection, rectification, and voltage transient suppression.
